Chinese Property Searching App Revamp

Overview

Juwai.com is an online property searching platform for Chinese real estate buyers to explore oversea properties. This project aims to improve the overall performance of the existing mobile app, to optimise user experience, increase the traffic and drive conversion.

Role: 95% UX Research，100% UI Design

Study Current Product

First of all, our team studied the usability and information architecture of the former app. Here are what we found from the key pages:

Stakeholder Interview

After we had a brief understanding of the current app, our team decided learn more from our stakeholders, so we interviewed them to see their goals and expectation of the revamp project in their own perspective.

Personas & User Journey

According to our user interviews, we learned that property buyers have different needs and processes based on their buying purpose. We summarised the background of various buyers, and designed 4 personas as representatives. After that, we drafted out their journey and analysed the pain points respectively.

Task Analysis

We analysed user journey maps, and grouped the pain points to investigate the actions/steps that users took to achieve their end goals.

IA & Wireframe

According to the task analysis, we drafted out a new information architecture for the mobile app and visualise the structure and features with wireframes. These diagrams were shown to stakeholders to make sure every team was on the same page before we proceeded to development stage.

Design System

Meanwhile, I started to define the design system for the app. For the colours and typography, we sticked to our brand identity guideline to ensure brand coherent among our products and platforms. Moreover, considered our app containing a lot of visual content and text, we decided to keep the overall UI simple and clean, to ensure our users could engage on the content without too much distraction on the screen. 

App Icon and Onboarding

The app icon is updated because our brand name is more recognisable than the chop logo, according to our user. Also we added onboarding pages to emphasise our brand values and the updates on our mobile app.
