Property Buying Motivation Landing Pages and Encyclopaedia

Overview

More than 70% of the users of came to Juwai.com by searching keywords related to their buying motives. Although we have so much content that useful to them but the content is not centralised and not easy to be found, resulting in the loss of users and the waste of content. Therefore, we hoped to improve the information architecture to optimise user's information searching experience and hence the consulting rate.

Role: 50% UX Research，100% UI Design

User research

According to our user data, more than 50% of users browse 1 page of the news article and leave our site. In order to understand the reason why user left our site,  we popped up a survey when they leave, the following picture showed the result of the survey. Based on the result, we set our goals for the project.

Analysis of target users' needs

Then, we re-visited the persona of Juwai.com to understand user goals and needs during the research phase of their property buying journey. This helped us to improve the current information architecture.

Card Sorting

I classify the reasons of user leaving our site from the survey result with the user needs to help generate the solution.

Ideation

Original Juwai.com was only a property search engine, it did not have features or pages that tailor for user's buying motivation. So we decided to develop new channels to fill this gap: Buying Motivation Landing pages and Encyclopaedia Channel.

Buying Motivation Landing pages
There are 4 new landing pages for user’s main property buying motivation which are investment, study abroad, immigration, Lifestyle. Each page consolidate related information to optimise user's searching experience. For example, for buyers who want to send their children to study aboard, they could find what they need on our Educational Landing Page, in terms of news, professionals, recommended property listings etc.

Encyclopaedia Channel
In addition to daily news, we would develop a new channel to re-organise the latest information, so user can find what they want easier. 

Information Architecture

The buying motivation landing pages and encyclopaedias will be connected to existing relevant pages in order to encourage users to keep browsing in our site.

Wireframe

I drafted wireframe to see how the information layout on the pages as well as the workflow.

Buying Motivation Landing Pages

We have designed property buying motivation landing pages in 4 themes — Investment, Education, Immigration, Lifestyle, which were designed with the same layout and structure but present different content according to the theme.

1/ Theme Logo
Logo shows the theme of the landing page – buyer's motivation; the font is consistent with Juwai logo to maintain visual coordination.

2/ Banner and Hot topics card
The banners and the cards below show popular news and topics that related to the theme of landing page

3/ Event Promotion
Provide users a channel to find professional and reputable organisations via events where they could interact directly with experts

4/ Property Recommendation
Based on the theme, recommending properties located in different countries

5/ Relevant policies
Policies that most consulted and browsed. Connect to encyclopaedia articles to improve enquiry rates

6/ Encyclopaedia/news
News related to the theme, sorted by latest time and connected to news/encyclopedia articles

Encyclopaedia Channel Homepage

The articles were categorised by property buying motivation and countries, this allows user to focus on the encyclopaedia information of their target location and preference.
